About this course

Built for a practitioner with 11 years of visual and UX design experience who wants a shared vocabulary — not beginner mechanics, but the reasoning framework behind decisions already made by instinct.

Each lesson skips the lecture and goes straight to the thinking: interactive wrong-way / right-way comparisons, expert drills, and real product interface examples. The exercises don't tell you what's wrong; you operate each interface and judge it yourself.

Source: Norman's The Design of Everyday Things · Interaction design principles
