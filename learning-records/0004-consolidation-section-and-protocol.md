# Feedback: end every lesson with a consolidation "strengthen the muscle" section

The learner praised the §08 interactive audit ("a great example of what I'm looking for") and asked that,
after the capstone, each lesson include an exercise to *reinforce the reasoning so it becomes conscious
and deliberate rather than instinctive*. He wants to internalise the thinking process, not just recognise it.

**Why it matters:** This is a request for storage strength + metacognition. Recognition quizzes give
fluency (illusory mastery); the durable goal is a procedure he runs on purpose. He's an instinctive
expert trying to make his instincts *explicit and defensible* — that's the whole mission.

**How to apply to every future lesson:**
- Add a final **"Strengthen the muscle"** section with three parts:
  1. **An explicit protocol** — the lesson's reasoning written as a numbered procedure he can run out loud,
     also saved as a print-friendly reference card in /reference.
  2. **A guided walk-through** — gated step-by-step drills where he must commit each reasoning step before
     the next unlocks (don't let him jump to the verdict), with at least two interleaved/mirror-image cases.
  3. **A real-world field mission** — go find N instances in apps he uses, logged in an autosaved form.
     Spacing + real-world = wisdom, and the natural hook for a follow-up critique conversation.
- The interactive screen-audit format (click rendered elements to flag, then auto-mark) is a proven hit —
  reuse it as the hard exercise in future lessons.

See [[0001-affordances-and-signifiers]] §13 and reference/diagnostic-protocol.html as the template.
