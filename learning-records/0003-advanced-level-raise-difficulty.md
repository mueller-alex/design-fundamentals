# Calibration: learner is advanced — pitch at judgment-call difficulty

The learner scored 100% on Lesson 01's first pass (classification + applied clinics) and asked for
harder material, explicitly noting strong ability. He also flagged that answer-option descriptions
that hint at the correct choice make exercises too easy — remove them.

**Why it matters:** Recognition- and single-rule-application-level exercises are below this learner's
zone of proximal development. The challenge has to come from *ambiguity and trade-offs*, not volume.

**How to apply going forward:**
- Default to **edge cases and contested judgment calls** where multiple answers are defensible and the
  reasoning (not the label) is what's graded. He responded well to the "when is a hidden affordance
  acceptable?" framing that *overturned* the lesson's own earlier simplification.
- Use 4-way+ classification with subtle distractors; avoid binary recognition.
- **Never** put give-away descriptions under graded answer options. Hints belong in post-answer feedback only.
- Introduce nuance that separates senior from junior reasoning (e.g. discoverability vs. total exposure,
  signifier *weight* matching action stakes, static decoration vs. interaction signifier).
