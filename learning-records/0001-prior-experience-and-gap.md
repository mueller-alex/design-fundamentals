# Established: expert executor, vocabulary/reasoning gap

The learner has 11 years of visual design experience (8 UX-focused) and a Digital Media & Design
degree. They can already *produce* good interfaces by instinct and empathy. The demonstrated gap is
**explicit design-thinking vocabulary and reasoning** — they cannot yet name or justify the principles
behind their choices.

**Implications for future sessions:**
- Do not teach UI mechanics or visual craft — that's mastered. Teach the *why* and the *language*.
- Anchor to Norman's canonical vocabulary first (affordance, signifier, mapping, feedback, gulfs,
  conceptual models), since that's the requested model and the field's foundation.
- The learner likely already *does* many of these things correctly; lessons should connect new terms
  to their existing instinct ("you already do this — here's its name") rather than build from zero.
- Preferred modality confirmed: interactive wrong-way/right-way comparisons. Lead with these.
