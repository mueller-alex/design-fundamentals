# Interaction & UX Design Resources

## Knowledge

- [Book: _The Design of Everyday Things_, Revised Ed. — Don Norman (2013)](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262525671/the-design-of-everyday-things/)
  The foundational text. Use for: affordances, signifiers, mapping, feedback, conceptual models, the seven stages of action, the gulfs of execution & evaluation. The whole mission orbits this book.
- [Article: "Affordances and Design" — Don Norman (jnd.org)](https://jnd.org/affordances_and_design/)
  Norman's own clarification of how the term "affordance" got misused, and why he introduced "signifier." Use for: the precise affordance-vs-signifier distinction, straight from the source.
- [Article: "Signifiers, Not Affordances" — Don Norman (jnd.org)](https://jnd.org/signifiers_not_affordances/)
  The essay where Norman introduces signifiers. Use for: why "perceived affordance" was a mistake and signifier is the better word.
- [Paper: "Technology Affordances" — William Gaver (CHI '91)](https://www.lri.fr/~mbl/Stanford/CS477/papers/Gaver-CHI1991.pdf)
  Introduces the 2×2 affordance matrix: perceptible / hidden / false affordance + correct rejection. Use for: diagnosing *which kind* of affordance failure an interface has. The applied backbone of Lesson 01.
- [Reference: "Seven Stages of Action" — Wikipedia (well-sourced summary)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_stages_of_action)
  Clean diagram and breakdown of goal → plan → specify → perform → perceive → interpret → compare. Use for: the action cycle and the two gulfs.
- [Article: "Natural Mappings and Stimulus–Response Compatibility" — NN/G](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/natural-mappings/)
  Applies Norman's mapping principle to UI; the stovetop logic in digital form. Use for: Lesson 02 mapping, spatial correspondence, when labels become unnecessary.
- [Article: "Response Times: The 3 Important Limits" — Jakob Nielsen (NN/G)](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/response-times-3-important-limits/)
  The 0.1s / 1s / 10s feedback thresholds. Use for: Lesson 02 feedback timing — which feedback a given latency demands.
- [Org: Nielsen Norman Group (nngroup.com)](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/)
  Evidence-based UX articles from Norman's own consultancy. Use for: applying foundational principles to concrete digital-product patterns. High trust, but prefer the primary book for the core theory.
